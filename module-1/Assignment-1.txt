Ripple Introduction

Course: Ripple Developer Course

Assignment Objective: The objective of this assignment is to introduce students to Ripple technology by exploring its history, primary use case in the financial industry, differences from traditional banking systems, and real-world adoption examples.

Instructions for Students:

Part 1: Research Ripple's History (20 points)

Research: Conduct research to provide a brief history of Ripple. Explore the following aspects:

When and by whom was Ripple founded?
What was the original motivation behind creating Ripple?
How has Ripple evolved over the years?
Timeline: Create a chronological timeline highlighting key milestones in Ripple's history.

Part 2: Primary Use Case of Ripple (20 points)

Use Case Explanation: Explain the primary use case of Ripple within the financial industry. Focus on the following aspects:
What problem or challenge does Ripple aim to solve?
How does Ripple technology address this problem or challenge?
Part 3: Differences from Traditional Banking Systems (20 points)

Comparison: Describe how Ripple differs from traditional banking systems. Consider the following points:

Transaction speed and cost.
Cross-border payments.
Interoperability with different currencies and financial institutions.
Advantages and Disadvantages: Discuss both the advantages and disadvantages of Ripple when compared to traditional banking systems.

Part 4: Real-World Adoption of Ripple (20 points)

Adoption Examples: Provide examples of financial institutions or companies that have adopted Ripple. Include information on:
Name of the institution or company.
How they have integrated Ripple into their operations.
The impact or benefits they have experienced as a result of using Ripple.
Part 5: Report Submission (20 points)

Report Format: Compile your research and findings into a well-structured report. Use clear headings and subheadings for each part of the assignment.

Citations: Ensure that you properly cite your sources using a recognized citation style (e.g., APA, MLA, Chicago).

Submission Guidelines:

Submit your completed assignment report as a PDF document through the course's learning management system (LMS) by the specified deadline.
Grading Rubric:

Completeness and accuracy of Ripple's history (20 points)
Clarity and depth of the use case explanation (20 points)
Thoroughness of the comparison with traditional banking systems (20 points)
Quality of examples and insights on real-world adoption (20 points)
Presentation and organization of the report (20 points)
